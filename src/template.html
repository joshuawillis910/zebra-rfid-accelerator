The 6-Step Workflow
Navigation is linear by default — each step feeds the next. You can jump to any step from the sidebar at any time. The progress bar reflects your position in the workflow. Completed steps are marked with a check.
STEP 1 — ENGAGEMENT SETUP
Capture company name, revenue, number of sites, seller info, and the discovery signal. The Key KPIs & Discovery Signal field is the most important — use the customer's own words. It appears verbatim on the PDF cover and drives the value narrative.
STEP 2 — INDUSTRY & VALUE LEVERS
Select one or more industries. All matching scenarios appear — deselect anything out of scope. Keep to 5–6 scenarios max for the sharpest conversation. Research shows more than 6 levers in a single meeting reduces buy-in.
STEP 3 — DISCOVERY INPUTS
Enter customer-specific values. Defaults are conservative benchmarks — replace as many as possible with real customer data. Every field shows a source hint (e.g. "GS1: 2–5 min"). Green indicators show which scenarios are fully populated.
STEP 4 — COST MODEL
Sliders set deployment assumptions — the line item table auto-builds below. Every cell is editable. Add custom BOM rows with + Add line item. The footer shows NRC, recurring, and 3-year TCO separately.
STEP 5 — ROI & VALUE CASE
Full financial model: 5-year NPV, IRR, payback chart, benefit ramp vs. cost curves, and the cost-of-delay slider. The per-scenario table shows Yr 1 ramped values — not full annual — reflecting the adoption ramp.
STEP 6 — FULL ANALYSIS
Customer-facing output. Export the 20+ page investment appraisal PDF, the 13-slide PPTX deck, or the one-page executive summary. JSON export saves state for handoffs.

Discovery Inputs
Each selected scenario has a set of input fields that drive its formula. Replace defaults with real customer data wherever possible — a model built on real numbers is materially more defensible than one built on benchmarks.
Benchmark Hints
Every field shows a grey hint below it — the source range and the default used. The default is always the conservative end of the range.
Loaded Labor Rate
Always use fully-loaded rate (wage + benefits + overhead) — typically 1.3–1.5× base wage. This is the number finance will verify, so use theirs if you have it.
Annual Revenue
Use revenue attributable to the sites in scope — not total group revenue. A 200-store deployment should use those stores' combined revenue, not the parent company's.
Objection Handlers
Each scenario includes a pre-written Challenge — a specific rebuttal tied to the formula logic and evidence. Use it when a customer pushes back on a specific number.
Cost Model
The cost model has two layers that work together. The top layer is a set of sliders — store count, readers per store, tags per item, labor rate, and a few others — that drive an automatic Bill of Materials estimate. This gives you a defensible baseline in under a minute without needing a formal quote. The numbers are derived from Zebra deployment benchmarks and update instantly as you adjust.

The bottom layer is a fully editable line-item table. Every auto-calculated row can be overridden — just click a cell and type. You can also add custom line items for anything the sliders don't cover (integration fees, customer-specific services, partner margins, etc.). Once you manually edit a row, it gets locked from slider updates so your changes are preserved.

For early-stage conversations, the slider defaults are usually enough to get to a credible payback number. For a CFO or procurement audience, replace the key rows with actual quote figures before exporting.
Cadence Options — How Costs Flow Through the Model
One-Time — Allocated to Year 0. Hardware, installation, services, training.
Annual — Repeats Year 1+. SaaS licenses, support, annual replenishment.
Monthly — Entered as monthly fee, annualised ×12, allocated Year 1+.
Per-Site/Year — Rate × qty (sites), allocated Year 1+. Useful for per-location contracts.
Other — Treated as one-time (Year 0). Use for custom one-off costs.
ℹ Manual Edit Flag — Once you edit a row or add a line item, an amber banner appears. Sliders will still update auto-calculated rows but won't overwrite your custom values. Use ↺ Reset & Recalc All in the banner to restore full slider control, or ↺ Reset to Example at the bottom of the page to reset everything including sliders.
The footer shows three totals — read them separately:
Non-Recurring (Yr 0)
One-time capital: hardware, tags, services, training + 7% contingency
Recurring (Yr 1+)
Annual operating cost from Year 1 forward: SaaS, replenishment, support
3-Year Total
Yr 0 + Yr 1 + (Yr 1 × 2). The TCO number finance uses for capital comparison

ROI & Value Case — Key Metrics
Annual Value (Yr 3+)
Fully-ramped run-rate benefit at steady state. Sum of all selected scenarios at 100% ramp. The number to quote when asked "what is this worth?"
Payback Period
Month in which cumulative net cash crosses zero. Shown on the cash position chart as a dashed line. If beyond 60 months, displayed as >60 mo.
5-Year NPV
Net Present Value discounted at 10%. Positive NPV = value creation above cost of capital. This is the primary metric for finance committee approval.
IRR
The discount rate at which NPV = 0. If IRR exceeds the customer's hurdle rate (typically 8–15%), the investment passes finance review at the headline level.
Cost of Delay
Slide 1–24 months to see cumulative value foregone while the decision is pending. Accurate — uses the same ramp curves as the main model. Use it to answer "what if we wait until next quarter?"
Yr 1 Ramped (in tables)
Not the full annual value — the Year 1 value after the benefit type ramp is applied. Always less than Full Annual. See ramp table above.

Evidence — Two Separate Systems
The tool uses two different evidence classifications. They are related but not the same thing — it's important to know which one you're looking at.
System 1 — Scenario Evidence Strength (●●● on each scenario card)
Appears on every scenario as a dot indicator + label. Reflects the overall weight of evidence backing that scenario's quantification — an aggregate judgment across all sources that support it.
STRENGTH
WHAT IT MEANS IN A CUSTOMER CONVERSATION
●●● Strong
Multiple independent studies corroborate the benchmark range, including at least one Tier 1 source. Safe to lead with in a CFO conversation. The number is defensible as-is.
●●○ Medium-Strong
Solid Tier 2 benchmark data with consistent directional findings across sources. Minor variability in the range. Use confidently — anchor to the customer's own data wherever possible to strengthen it further.
●○○ Emerging
Practitioner data or early-stage studies. Real directional signal but narrower evidence base. Use as a supporting scenario, not the anchor. The customer's own operational data matters more here than the benchmark.
System 2 — Source Tier (Tier 1/2/3 in the Evidence Registry)
Appears in the Evidence Registry (sidebar). Classifies each individual published source by who produced it and the rigour of their methodology — independent of how many scenarios that source backs.
TIER
SOURCE TYPE
CITATION CREDIBILITY
Tier 1
Academic / Government
Peer-reviewed research or official government report. Examples: Auburn University RFID Lab, AORN, GAO-25-107427. Citable in board-level presentations without qualification.
Tier 2
Industry Benchmark
Major industry body, analyst firm, or multi-customer manufacturer study. Examples: ECR Community, NRF, Gartner, Aberdeen Group, ECRI Institute, GE Healthcare. Solid methodology, verifiable range.
Tier 3
Practitioner / Operator
Vendor white paper, trade association data, or practitioner case study. Examples: GS1 US deployment guides, Zebra / VDC Research. Strong directional signal — best used alongside Tier 1/2 as corroboration.
ℹ
How they relate: A scenario tagged ●●● Strong typically has a Tier 1 source backing it — but what makes it Strong is that multiple sources agree. A scenario can be Medium-Strong even with a Tier 1 source if that study only partially covers the use case. And a scenario backed only by Tier 3 sources will always be Emerging regardless of how reputable that Tier 3 publisher is. Scenario strength is the aggregate; source tier is per-citation.

Field Tips
Build the hard case first
Select 3–4 Hard Labor and Hard Cost scenarios only. If that alone shows positive ROI with payback under 24 months, you have a floor. Revenue and working capital scenarios are all upside from there.
Use the customer's exact numbers
Shrink %, loaded labor rate, and inventory value are almost always available directly from the customer's P&L or controller. Real numbers make the case materially stronger — and much harder to challenge.
Use the Cost of Delay slider live
In a meeting, pull the slider to 3 months when a customer says "let's revisit next quarter." Show the number. Let them do the math. It is a direct calculation, not an estimate.
Don't include Strategic scenarios in the headline
Strategic scenarios (data foundation, AI, digital twin) are real but slow. Including them in a headline ROI without prior alignment invites challenge and can undermine harder, faster scenarios.
IRR is your insurance number
If IRR exceeds the customer's hurdle rate, the investment passes finance review at the headline level. The scenario-by-scenario debate becomes secondary.
Scope revenue to the right unit
Use revenue attributable to the sites in scope — not total group or parent company revenue. A 200-store deployment should use those stores' revenue, not the holding company's.
